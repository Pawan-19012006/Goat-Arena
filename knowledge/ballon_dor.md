# Ballon d'Or History & Statistics

## Award Overview
- Established in 1956 by French news magazine *France Football*.
- Awarded annually to the best performing player over the season (previously calendar year).
- Historically open only to European players, expanded to all players playing in Europe in 1995, and global in 2007.

## All-Time Winners Leaderboard
- **Lionel Messi**: 8 awards (2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2015, 2019, 2021, 2023) - Argentina / Barcelona / PSG / Inter Miami.
- **Cristiano Ronaldo**: 5 awards (2008, 2013, 2014, 2016, 2017) - Portugal / Manchester United / Real Madrid.
- **Michel Platini**: 3 awards (1983, 1984, 1985) - France / Juventus.
- **Johan Cruyff**: 3 awards (1971, 1973, 1974) - Netherlands / Ajax / Barcelona.
- **Marco van Basten**: 3 awards (1988, 1989, 1992) - Netherlands / AC Milan.
- **Franz Beckenbauer**: 2 awards (1972, 1976) - Germany / Bayern Munich.
- **Ronaldo Nazário**: 2 awards (1997, 2002) - Brazil / Inter Milan / Real Madrid.
- **Alfredo Di Stéfano**: 2 awards (1957, 1959) - Spain / Real Madrid.
- **Kevin Keegan**: 2 awards (1978, 1979) - England / Hamburg.
- **Karl-Heinz Rummenigge**: 2 awards (1980, 1981) - Germany / Bayern Munich.

## Key Facts
- Lionel Messi is the only player to win the award across three different decades (2000s, 2010s, 2020s).
- Cristiano Ronaldo holds the record for the most nominations in history (18 nominations).
- Pelé was awarded an honorary Ballon d'Or (Prix d'Honneur) in 2013, as South Americans were ineligible during his active years.
