# Origins
Erling Haaland was born in 2000 in Leeds, England, to former Norwegian defender Alfie Haaland. He spent his youth development at Norwegian club Bryne FK.

# History
Haaland signed with Molde FK in 2017 under Ole Gunnar Solskjaer. He moved to Red Bull Salzburg in 2019, Borussia Dortmund in 2020, and transferred to Manchester City in 2022.

# Country
Haaland represents the Norway National Team.

# Club Career
Bryne (2015–2016), Molde (2017–2018), Red Bull Salzburg (2019–2020), Borussia Dortmund (2020–2022), and Manchester City (2022–present).

# Records
Haaland holds the record for most goals in a single Premier League season (36 goals in 2022-23). He is the fastest player to score 30 UEFA Champions League goals (in 25 games) and the youngest to reach 40 goals.

# Achievements
Haaland won the continental treble (UEFA Champions League, Premier League, FA Cup) with Manchester City in 2022-23. He won the Premier League Golden Boot in 2023 and 2024, and the DFB-Pokal with Dortmund in 2021.

# Managers
Haaland played under Ole Gunnar Solskjaer, Marco Rose, and Pep Guardiola.

# Major Tournaments
Represented Norway in UEFA Nations League and Euro qualifiers. He has not played in a senior World Cup or Euro final tournament.

# Recent Form
Haaland won the Premier League Golden Boot in 2023-24 (27 goals) and helped City secure another Premier League title. He started the 2024-25 season scoring multiple hat-tricks.

# Strengths
Unmatched physical acceleration, elite off-ball movement, exceptional one-touch finishing, and raw strength inside the penalty area.

# Weaknesses
Extremely low touch counts, minimal contribution to team build-up play, and can be marked out of games by deep defensive blocks.

# Debate Points
Haaland is the most efficient, pure goalscorer in the world, breaking Premier League records in his debut season and securing a treble.

# Counter Points
Point out that Haaland has never played in a major international tournament for Norway, and relies heavily on service from City's elite midfielders.
