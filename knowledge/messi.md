# Origins
Lionel Messi was born in 1987 in Rosario, Argentina. Diagnosed with a growth hormone deficiency as a child, his local club career began at Newell's Old Boys before Barcelona signed him.

# History
Messi moved to Barcelona's La Masia in 2000. He made his senior Barcelona debut in 2004, playing 17 seasons at the club. He transferred to Paris Saint-Germain in 2021, and to Inter Miami in 2023.

# Country
Messi represents the Argentina National Team, serving as the long-term captain.

# Club Career
Messi spent his peak club career at Barcelona (2004–2021), followed by PSG (2021–2023), and currently plays for Inter Miami in Major League Soccer (2023–present).

# Records
Messi holds the record for most goals in a single calendar year (91 goals in 2012). He is the all-time leading scorer in La Liga history (474 goals) and has the most career assists in football history (over 380 assists).

# Achievements
Messi has won a record 8 Ballon d'Or awards (2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2015, 2019, 2021, 2023). He has claimed 44 major trophies, including the 2022 FIFA World Cup, 2 Copa Américas (2021, 2024), and 4 Champions Leagues.

# Managers
Messi played under Frank Rijkaard, Pep Guardiola, Luis Enrique, Gerardo Martino, and Lionel Scaloni.

# Major Tournaments
Messi won the FIFA World Cup in 2022 (winning the Golden Ball twice, 2014 and 2022). He won the Copa América in 2021 and 2024, and the 2008 Olympics.

# Recent Form
Messi led Inter Miami to their first Leagues Cup title in 2023 and won the 2024 Copa América with Argentina. He continues scoring and creating goals at a high rate in MLS.

# Strengths
Elite progressive dribbling, defense-splitting pocket passes, set-piece accuracy, and low goal-to-shot ratio. Extremely high football intelligence and space awareness.

# Weaknesses
Low defensive work rate, minimal pressing involvement, and age-related decline in absolute sprinting pace.

# Debate Points
Messi is the complete football package, leading in both all-time goal contribution rates and playmaker assists, capped by the 2022 World Cup.

# Counter Points
Point out that Messi's Champions League knockout record post-2015 includes heavy collapses, and that he spent his entire peak in a single system at Barcelona.
