# Origins
Cristiano Ronaldo was born in 1985 in Madeira, Portugal. He started his youth football at Andorinha and Nacional before joining Sporting CP's academy.

# History
Ronaldo signed with Manchester United in 2003 under Sir Alex Ferguson. In 2009, he joined Real Madrid for a world-record €94m. He moved to Juventus in 2018, returned to United in 2021, and joined Al-Nassr in 2023.

# Country
Ronaldo represents the Portugal National Team, serving as the team captain.

# Club Career
Sporting CP (2002–2003), Manchester United (2003–2009, 2021–2022), Real Madrid (2009–2018), Juventus (2018–2021), and Al-Nassr in the Saudi Pro League (2023–present).

# Records
Ronaldo is the all-time top scorer in international football (130+ goals) and the Champions League (140 goals). He holds the record for most goals in a single UCL campaign (17 goals in 2013-14) and has scored over 890 career goals.

# Achievements
Ronaldo has won 5 Ballon d'Or awards (2008, 2013, 2014, 2016, 2017). He has won 33 major trophies, including 5 UEFA Champions Leagues, 3 Premier Leagues, 2 La Ligas, 2 Serie A titles, UEFA Euro 2016, and the 2019 Nations League.

# Managers
Ronaldo played under Sir Alex Ferguson, Jose Mourinho, Carlo Ancelotti, Zinedine Zidane, and Fernando Santos.

# Major Tournaments
Ronaldo led Portugal to their first international trophies: UEFA Euro 2016 and the 2019 Nations League. He has scored in 5 different World Cup editions.

# Recent Form
Ronaldo won the Saudi Pro League Golden Boot in 2023-24, scoring 35 goals in a single season for Al-Nassr, and continues to captain Portugal.

# Strengths
Incredible athletic longevity, elite aerial duel success, off-ball movement, penalty box positioning, and dual-foot finishing power.

# Weaknesses
Zero involvement in defensive pressing and build-up recovery, decline in completed dribbles, and dependency on service from wingers.

# Debate Points
Ronaldo is the ultimate goalscorer in football history, dominant in three different major European leagues, and holds unmatched Champions League knockout records.

# Counter Points
Argue that Ronaldo has never won a World Cup knockout stage match or claimed a World Cup trophy, and that his goal tally is heavily boosted by penalty volumes.
